The Origin

Everyone likes a good origin story whether it be a character in a movie or an invention that is preparing to change the world.

Let's go back to the 1930s when digital computers were the talk of the town or should I say "world".

In 1931, Kurt Godel encoded mathematical statements and proofs as integers and showed that there are true theorems that are unprovable by any consistent theorem-proving machine, computer programs which proves theorems are called "theorem-proving machine". And using mathematical statements and proof Kurt was able to identified limits of algorithmic theorem proving, computing, and any type of computation-based AI," eventually laying the foundations for Theoretical computer science [1] and AI theory [2].

In 1935, Alonzo Church, an American mathematician extended Godel's proof and explained that the decision problem of computer science doesn't have a general solution. He later developed Lambda calculus, which will eventually be fundamental to the theory of computer languages and it helps in defining a set of rules for how computations work. Like Alonzo and Kurt, there was another person who agreed with them.

Turing Proof

Turing Proof by Alan Turing (often called as "father of modern computer science or AI") another renowned British mathematician and computer scientist published a proof in 1936 which in simple words states there are certain problems that a computer cannot solve even if they are very smart. There are decision problems that are undecidable and no computer can say "yes" or "no" as an answer like The Halting Problem, which is the problem of determining whether a computer program will finish running/working or will it continue to run/work forever. Even though this proof might seem like demotivation but it is actually a game changer, because this proof saved so much time for researchers and encouraged them to focus on developing an algorithm that can achieve achievable goals rather than trying to find an answer to a question that has no definite answer. In simple words again, "go around the rock which is blocking your way rather than spending time in breaking it". Well, you may ask how did the proof help us? It helped us understand the limitations of AI and use creative ways to design an AI

Fact: 1941 built the first working program-controlled general-purpose computer

Also first mathematical description of an artificial neural network was mentioned in "A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity (1943)" by Warren Sturgis and Walter Pitts. Which we know paved it's way into Machine Learning.


Cybernetics (first step to AI that we know): Call me AI from now on, ok


The term Cybernetics was coined around 1943 by Norbert Wiener, Arturo Rosenblueth, and Julian Bigelow. Cybernetics is an amazing process that contributed a lot to the development of AI. In simple words, if I explain it is a circular feedback system where the computer's output is it's input, going in a loop while the computer corrects it's mistake which also laid the foundation of AI's, ability to understand information in diiferent new ways and they correct themselves through feedbacks from it's own outputs. This feature of mordern AI revolves around this process called Cybernetics.


Game Theory: Understanding AI in a new way

Game theory has a big role in AI development. Game Theory, not the MatPat one but instead by mathematician John Von Neumann and economist Oskar Morgenstern, was introduced with the 1944 paper "Theory of Games and Economic Behavior." It is a mathematical model of strategic interactions; for example, if you are playing a two-player game if enough data is gathered from different interactions of the game played by you is given to the computer, it can predict your next move using math and statistics. It has applications in various fields like social science, economics, logic, and computer science. If you read the previous sentence, you will understand that game theory's main point is to predict the next move of something. Many AI tools we use revolve around this theory, like weather forecasts, AI uses years of weather data to predict whether it will rain or not. Like that, only chess bots could predict your next move by analyzing your previous moves and understanding your pattern.
